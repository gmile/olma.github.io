21.05.2020

Unanchored line. It flies. Somebody can think that it is crawling, somebody can think, it is going slowly step by step, making very small step at a time, somebody can think that it is running, somebody can think that it is swimming in the space of water following precise direction. But it is flying. There is no gravity around. It is alone. It doesn't want to stop, but it doesn’t want to be very quick. It doesn’t want to want anymore. It only wants to fly. And it continues. It make circles, it repeats actions and patterns, it finds new patterns. It looses strength. It finds strength. It doesn’t understand how though. It continues. It flies. It has fear. It has fear to be unable to stop, or to stop forever, or to stop flying, or to start flying fast. Sometimes it has a lot of fear. But then it remembers its lightness, it remembers its sense. It continues.

It meets other realities. Forms. They are creation of lines, or lines are creation of forms. They meet. Some forms are also flying and it is good and easy to fly together. Some forms trying to disrupt line, to swallow it, to absorb it, to deform it, to stop it, to anchor it, to eat it, to dissolve it. Line has strength. Line continues. Line now has bad memories, line is thicker, line is tired, line remembers its fear. Line continues. Line flies.

And line doesn’t know when the stone will let it go.
